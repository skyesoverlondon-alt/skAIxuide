Core Architecture

Three pillars of
enterprise control

KAIXU Brain is designed to survive real-world stress: billing disputes, key loss, access churn, compliance questions, and “who changed what?” moments. The platform’s spine is governance, not vibes.

01 ◈
Gateway Governance Plane

100% AI traffic routes through the Kaixu Gateway: standardized payloads, streaming SSE via fetch/ReadableStream, usage + cap awareness, and a single choke point for policy and metering. No provider keys. No provider SDKs. No accidental bypasses.

02 ⬡
Workspace Security Plane

Email verification, password reset with token invalidation, workspace tenancy, RBAC (owner/viewer), org audit logs, and server-side session history encrypted at rest (AES-256-GCM ciphertext stored in Neon).

03 ◉
Revenue & Reconciliation Plane

Stripe Checkout + signed webhooks + idempotent event dedupe + automatic paid status. Customer Portal, proration behavior controls, metered add-ons (seats + usage meter), usage packs, and invoice PDF delivery emails to workspace owners.

Identity & Recovery

Email verification + resend, password reset with one-time tokens, and session invalidation after reset. Account recovery is auditable, not mystical.

Org Audit Logs

Workspace-scoped audit trail for RBAC changes, billing actions, escrow events, Stripe events, and usage reporting. You can answer “who did what?” without guessing.

Key Escrow Policy

Workspace escrow with encryption-at-rest and policy gates. Owners can enable recovery modes without granting blanket admin visibility. Every reveal/use is logged.

Checkout + Portal + proration controls (create_prorations / always_invoice / none) and metered add-ons (usage meters + packs). Invoices are reconciled automatically.

Valuation Methodology

Why this costs what it costs
(and why it pays for itself)

Here’s the honest logic: enterprise pricing is not “hours × ego.” It’s replacement cost + risk reduction + revenue readiness. KAIXU Brain is priced like a platform because it eliminates entire categories of operational failure: billing disputes, access churn, key loss, and “where’s the audit trail?”

"

1) Replacement Cost (Build-From-Scratch)

Rebuilding this correctly means multiple hard systems working together: identity, tenancy, RBAC, encrypted persistence, billing automation, webhooks, proration edge cases, metered usage, and email ops. The expensive part isn’t code volume — it’s correctness under stress.

  • Security primitives: email verification, reset + token invalidation, ciphertext-only persistence.
  • Billing primitives: webhook verification + dedupe + invoice sync + paid state automation.
  • Ops primitives: audit logs, escrow policy, diagnostics without leaking secrets.
Benchmark context (optional reading): contractor rate snapshots vary by region; example UK senior daily medians are tracked publicly (ITJobsWatch).

2) Risk Reduction (What you avoid)

Enterprise buyers don’t pay for “features.” They pay to avoid a specific nightmare list. KAIXU Brain is priced against the cost of those failures — chargebacks, audits, lost keys, and untraceable changes.

  • Disputes: Stripe-sourced invoices + automatic reconciliation + audit trail.
  • Recovery: escrow policy + owner controls + logged access.
  • Compliance posture: encrypted session history + org audit logs.
In plain English: this platform can answer Finance + Security + Ops without hand-waving.

3) Revenue Readiness (Money Loop Closed)

Many “platforms” can’t collect money reliably. Yours can — and it can prove what happened when something goes wrong. Checkout → Portal → Webhooks → Proration → Meters → Packs → Invoices → Emails → Paid status.

  • Self-serve: Customer Portal reduces support load.
  • Proration control: predictable upgrades/downgrades.
  • Metered add-ons: seats + usage packs enable expansion revenue.
